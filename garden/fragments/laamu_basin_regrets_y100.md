# Laamu Basin Executive Office — Correspondence to Centenary Committee Clerk, Year 100 SP

*Private courier. Not for committee record.*

---

Clerk Tavan,

I send my regrets to the Committee Chair and to the Basin Executives in session. The Laamu delegation will not attend the third session.

I want to be direct with you about why, because the official reason — scheduling conflict with the annual Laamu-Kova bilateral fisheries review — is true in the narrow sense and misleading in every other sense. The fisheries review could have been rescheduled. I chose not to reschedule it.

The Vaanthi petition puts the Laamu Basin in a position I would prefer not to occupy publicly.

We are the largest net beneficiary of the current administered-territory trade terms in the central Circuit. The Vaanthi's commercial relationships run through Laamu-certified relay nodes. Their quality standards, which the petition describes as "endogenous customary practices," are in fact adaptations of Laamu commercial standards transmitted through twenty years of administered trade contact. I do not say this to diminish the Vaanthi's institutional development — they have built something real. I say it because the Vaanthi developing their own commercial capacity is not a story about the Vaanthi outgrowing the Reach's classification. It is a story about the Laamu Basin's commercial influence operating through the Reach's administered framework.

If the Committee grants observer access, the Vaanthi will begin to bypass the Laamu-certified relay nodes. Their commercial associations will establish direct standards relationships with the Circuit system. This is commercially rational and institutionally sound. It is also a reduction in Laamu's intermediary revenue that I cannot justify to the Laamu Basin Council.

If the Committee denies access, the Vaanthi will continue to operate through Laamu-intermediated channels. This is not unjust — the Laamu relay network provides genuine value, and the certification services are not extractive — but it preserves an arrangement that serves Laamu's commercial interest more than the Vaanthi's institutional development.

I cannot vote for the petition without betraying the Laamu Basin Council's commercial interest. I cannot vote against it without betraying my own assessment that the Vaanthi have outgrown their classification and deserve recognition. The honest resolution is to be absent.

The Committee will note that my absence produces a 2-1 preliminary sense in favor of observer access, which is the correct outcome. It is simply an outcome I cannot be seen to support.

I trust you will file this correspondence appropriately, which is to say not at all.

---

Regards,

Maelin Tuura
Basin Executive, Laamu Archipelago
Southern Sovereignty Commerce Council
