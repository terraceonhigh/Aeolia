# ADMIRALTY DISPATCH — YEAR ZERO (RECONSTRUCTED)

*From the recovered signals archive of the Reach Fleet Intelligence Bureau.*  
*Translated from operational cipher. Marginalia in original hand.*  
*Classification status: declassified under the 50-year rule.*

---

**TRANSMISSION 7741-ALPHA**  
**Origin: Deep Survey Station Kelvari-7**  
**Received: Tick 391, Year -100 BP**  
**Distribution: Fleet Admiral / First Minister ONLY**

---

To the Admiral's Bureau:

At 0340 local, Station Kelvari-7 intercepted structured radio traffic
on the southern octave bands. Frequency: 18.4 MHz. Bandwidth: narrow-
cast. Interval: regular, 47-second periods. Duration of reception window:
eleven minutes before signal faded.

The traffic is not random noise. Spectral analysis shows deliberate
encoding. The signal-to-noise ratio at this distance is consistent with
a transmitter of industrial-era power — not coastal communication
equipment. The pattern is not one we recognize from any known relay
station or merchant fleet.

Cross-reference with the seismic record: Station Kelvari-7 has logged
four anomalous sub-surface events in the past two years, originating
from a bearing consistent with the radio source. Yield estimates range
from 0.8 to 1.4 kilotons. Previous assessment attributed these to
tectonic activity in the Abyssal Chain. This assessment is now in
question.

We are not the only ones who can split the atom.

I want to be clear about what I am and am not saying. I am not saying
that war is imminent. I am not saying that the signal represents a
hostile party. I am not even certain that the signal represents what I
believe it represents.

I am saying that if it does, the strategic situation as understood for
the past forty years is no longer the strategic situation.

*[Marginal notation, different hand: "Confirmed. See Kelvari-9 intercept,
same bearing, received 16 hours later. They know we're here too. — V.K."]*

The Admiral's Bureau will understand the implications better than I can
express in cipher. I will only note that the second-strike capability
of our southern fleet assumes that the origin of any first strike is
known in advance. It does not assume that the origin is unknown and
potentially symmetric in capability.

We have been operating under a specific model of strategic isolation.
That model may no longer be valid.

I recommend the following, for the Admiral's Bureau's consideration:

1. Satellite constellation reorientation to southern coverage within 90 days.
2. Quiet review of second-strike doctrine for the case of unknown-origin
   detection.
3. No communication to the Ministry of Trade. The relay partners must not
   know that we have changed our threat assessment. Their neutrality is
   worth more than their intelligence.

*[Marginal notation: "Agreed on (3). V.K."]*

Awaiting instructions.

*Commander Ilse Farag-Vann*  
*Station Kelvari-7, Deep Survey Command*

---

**TRANSMISSION 7742-ALPHA (ADDENDUM)**  
**Received: Tick 391, Year -100 BP, 6 hours later**

One additional observation which I omitted from the previous transmission
and which I believe the Admiral's Bureau should have before any decisions
are made.

The signal we received is not identical to our own encoding protocols.
It is *analogous* to them. The 47-second periodicity is not a standard
Reach Fleet interval — it is approximately 12% longer than our own
heartbeat signal.

I have spent the past six hours considering what this means.

The most likely explanation is that whoever is transmitting arrived at
similar solutions to similar problems from a different starting point.
They needed to maintain station-keeping synchronization across a deep-
ocean relay network. We needed the same thing. We arrived at 42-second
heartbeats. They arrived at 47-second heartbeats.

We solved the same problem independently.

I do not know whether to find this reassuring or terrifying. I have
decided that it is simply true.

*Commander Farag-Vann*

were deployed. The relay networks, maintained by intermediate-belt
polities throughout the nuclear era, proved essential to post-contact
trade stability — precisely as Commander Farag-Vann predicted in
clause (3) of her recommendation.*
