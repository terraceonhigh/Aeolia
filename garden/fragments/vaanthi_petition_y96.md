# Petition of the Vaanthi Basin Commercial Association to the Commerce Council Secretariat, Year 96 SP

*Filed under the Circuit's External Commercial Relations protocol. Received and acknowledged by the Secretariat. Pending review.*

---

To the Honorable Members of the Commerce Council Secretariat, the Research Directorate, and the Standing Committee on External Commercial Relations —

The Vaanthi Basin Commercial Association writes in advance of the Commerce Council's centenary proceedings, which we understand will include a comprehensive review of the Circuit system's first hundred years. We write not as Circuit members — the Vaanthi Basin does not hold Circuit membership and is not, under current conditions, eligible for it — but as a commercial community that has operated within the Circuit's influence for the entire period under review, and whose experience may be relevant to the Council's assessment.

---

## Our Position

The Vaanthi Basin is classified, under the Reach's administrative taxonomy, as an Administered Territory — Class II (partial commercial autonomy, metropolitan-set export terms, no independent relay certification authority). This classification was assigned at the time of the Reach's initial administration of the Basin, which predates the Strange Peace by approximately four hundred years. The classification has not been modified in the intervening period.

Under Class II status, Vaanthi commercial operators may trade within the Reach's administered relay network at terms set by the Reach's Civil Registry directorate. They may not certify cargo independently for relay transit outside the administered network. They may not petition for Circuit membership, because Circuit membership requires either independent sovereignty or Reach associate status, and Class II territories hold neither.

The Reach has been transitioning administered territories to associate status since approximately Year 82 SP. Eleven territories have completed the transition. The Vaanthi Basin has not been offered the transition. We have been informed, through the Civil Registry's Basin Liaison Office, that the transition pathway is "under consideration" and that "commercial readiness assessments are ongoing."

We have been informed of this for nine years.

---

## What We Observe

The Commerce Council's centenary review will, we expect, document the Circuit system's considerable achievements: relay access, arbitration governance, the mutual recognition framework, the growth of membership from nine to thirty-one basins.

We observe these achievements from a specific position. We observe them as a community that buys Circuit-certified goods at Circuit prices, that sells paddi into Circuit relay networks at terms set by an administrative directorate located four thousand sea-leagues from our shores, and that has no formal voice in the standards that govern either transaction.

We do not contest that the Circuit system has worked. We observe that it has worked, in part, because communities like ours have supplied the bulk caloric base that the Circuit's relay commerce processes and distributes. The char that the Sargasso basin processes and marks up — the char that provides the Sargasso's relay margin and that the Commerce Council's economic reporting proudly cites as evidence of the Circuit's commercial sophistication — is grown on hillside plots forty leagues south of here by cultivators who have never seen the inside of a Commerce Council proceeding and whose export terms are set in a Civil Registry office they have never visited.

We do not say this to diminish the Circuit's achievements. We say it because the centenary review should account for the full commercial system, including the portions of it that the Circuit did not build and does not govern but that the Circuit's prosperity depends upon.

---

## What We Ask

We ask three things, in order of their practicality.

First, we ask that the Secretariat's centenary review include, in its assessment of the Circuit's commercial performance, a section on the commercial conditions of the administered territories that supply the Circuit's bulk caloric inputs. The review should document the terms-of-trade differential between Circuit-member basins and administered territories, which our own records indicate has widened, not narrowed, since the Year 77 Joint Commercial Standards Protocol.

Second, we ask that the Commerce Council develop a formal pathway for administered-territory communities to participate in Commerce Council standards-setting proceedings as observers, without requiring Circuit membership. The standards that the Commerce Council sets for relay certification, cargo grading, and commercial dispute arbitration affect our commerce directly. We should not be setting those standards. We should be able to observe the process by which they are set.

Third, we ask that the Commerce Council communicate to the Reach's Civil Registry directorate the Council's view on the administered-territory-to-associate transition timeline. We understand that this transition is a matter of Reach internal governance and not the Commerce Council's jurisdiction. We also understand that the Commerce Council's centenary proceedings will occur in the same year that the Reach's Civil Registry is conducting its own internal review of the administered-territory system. The timing is an opportunity. A statement from the Council — even an informal one, even a procedural one — indicating the Council's interest in a more inclusive commercial geography would be heard by the parties whose decisions determine whether communities like ours remain permanently outside the system that the centenary will celebrate.

---

## Closing

We are aware that this petition arrives at the Secretariat with no institutional weight behind it. The Vaanthi Basin Commercial Association is a voluntary organization of sixty-two commercial houses, not a polity or a recognized governance body. We hold no Circuit membership and no Circuit vote. We have no leverage.

We have the observation that the Circuit system's second century will be built on the same commercial geography as its first, and that the communities who supply the system's material base should not be permanently invisible to the institutions that govern it.

We request acknowledgment of this petition and inclusion in the Secretariat's review process at whatever level the Committee on External Commercial Relations considers appropriate.

Respectfully submitted,

**The Vaanthi Basin Commercial Association**
Year 96 of the Strange Peace
