# Internal Memorandum: Anomalous Revenue Decline, Oru Basin Administrative Station

*Basin Revenue Office, Oru Basin Administered Territory (Class II). Marked for Civil Registry review. Year 67 SP.*

---

To: Civil Registry Directorate, Revenue Assessment Division
From: Oru Basin Revenue Office, Station Administrator Kenat Voss
Re: Quarterly extraction shortfall — request for guidance

---

The Oru Basin Revenue Office reports a sixty-two percent decline in assessed revenue transfer to the metropolitan account for the current quarter. This decline is not attributable to any identifiable local condition. Paddi yields remain within normal variance. Fishery landings are adequate. No epidemic event has been reported. The commercial relay is operating at expected volume. Population and cultivated area are stable.

We have reviewed the Revenue Assessment Division's standard diagnostic checklist (Form 14-C, "Revenue Shortfall Investigation"):

- **Crop failure?** No. Primary yield within 4% of five-year average.
- **Trade disruption?** No. Relay volume unchanged; three Circuit-certified transits completed this quarter.
- **Population decline?** No. Census estimate stable at 12,400 ± 300.
- **Local resistance or non-compliance?** No active resistance reported. The Basin Council continues to cooperate with assessment procedures. Compliance is, if anything, improved — the Council's new chair has been notably more responsive to Civil Registry communications than his predecessor.
- **Administrative error?** Reviewed. No calculation errors identified in our assessment methodology. The shortfall appears in the *assessed rate itself*, which has been adjusted downward by the metropolitan formula. We did not make this adjustment. It arrived in the standard quarterly rate-setting dispatch.

We do not know why the assessed rate was reduced. The dispatch did not include explanatory documentation. The rate-setting formula is maintained by the Revenue Assessment Division and is not disclosed to field stations.

---

## Observed Local Effects

The revenue reduction has had immediate consequences in the Basin that the Directorate should be aware of:

**The Basin Council has interpreted the reduction as a policy signal.** Chair Davan Sorel stated in the open council session that the metropolitan administration "appears to have recognized the Basin's capacity for self-sustaining commerce" and that the Council should "prepare for the responsibilities that follow." We have not confirmed or denied this interpretation because we do not know whether it is correct.

**Local reinvestment has accelerated.** The paddi surplus that would normally have been transferred to the metropolitan account is being directed by the Council toward harbor maintenance, a fish-drying facility, and what Chair Sorel describes as a "commercial readiness assessment" — Basin-funded, not Civil Registry-directed. We are uncertain whether this reinvestment is authorized under Class II terms. The relevant regulation (Administrative Order 7-B, §14.2) prohibits "independent capital formation exceeding subsistence requirements" but does not define "subsistence requirements" for a Basin of this size and commercial activity.

**Morale among Basin residents is measurably improved.** This is not a formal assessment metric but it is observable. The market is busier. The fishing cooperatives have expanded their pre-dawn schedule. Two new qahwa houses have opened near the harbor. The Council has begun referring to the reduced extraction as "the new terms."

---

## Request

We request clarification from the Revenue Assessment Division on three questions:

1. Is the rate reduction deliberate policy, a formula adjustment, or an error?
2. Should the Basin Council's interpretation — that this represents a transition signal — be confirmed, corrected, or left unaddressed?
3. Is the local reinvestment activity authorized under existing Class II terms, or should we issue a corrective notice?

We note that if the Council's interpretation is incorrect, the longer we wait to clarify, the more disruptive the correction will be. Chair Sorel has begun drafting what he calls a "readiness dossier" for Civil Registry review. If the rate reduction is an error, the dossier will be the source of considerable embarrassment — the Council's and ours.

If the rate reduction is deliberate, we would appreciate knowing, so that we can support the transition rather than watching it happen around us.

---

*[Second marginal annotation, same hand, later date:]* Reclassified this station's query as routine. Basin Council's "readiness dossier" received by Administered Territory Affairs and placed in the transition pipeline.
