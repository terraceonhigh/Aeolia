# Commerce Council Centenary Committee — Third Session Record, Year 100 SP

*Maret-on-the-Reef, Southern Sovereignty. Recorded by Committee Clerk. Basin Executive use only.*

---

**ATTENDING:** Committee Chair (Maret Basin); Basin Executives from Kova, Haugen, Peloro; Commerce Council Secretariat Deputy; Reach Civil Registry Liaison (observer, non-voting)

**SUBJECT:** Petition of the Vaanthi Basin Commercial Association for Observer Access to the Circuit Standards System

**DOCUMENT REFERENCE:** Vaanthi Petition VCA/Y96-001, received Year 96 SP, referred to Centenary Committee Year 99 SP

---

## Chair's Opening Remarks

The Chair notes that this petition has been before the Secretariat for four years. The Centenary Committee's mandate is to review it together with seven other outstanding petitions and institutional questions that the regular Commerce Council calendar has been unable to accommodate. The centenary is an appropriate occasion. The Chair asks the Committee to address three questions:

1. Does the Commerce Council have standing to consider a petition from a Reach-administered territory?
2. If standing exists, what precedent does a favorable response create?
3. If standing does not exist, what response, if any, does the Committee recommend?

---

## Discussion Summary

**Kova Basin Executive** opens by noting that the Kova Archipelago was itself an administered territory under Reach Class II classification until Year 74 SP, when the transition to Sovereignty associate status was negotiated under the bilateral framework. The Kova Executive states that the question of standing is not abstract for the Kova delegation: the petition before the Committee is from a territory whose institutional position is substantially similar to Kova's position twenty-six years ago.

The Kova Executive's position is that standing exists. The Circuit Standards system is a commercial framework, not a political one. The Commerce Council has accepted observer status for non-member commercial entities since Year 34 SP (the Thalassa Protocol). A commercial association — which is what the Vaanthi petitioners describe themselves as — falls within the precedent. The fact that the association's members are Reach-administered subjects is not, in the Kova Executive's assessment, a disqualifying condition.

**Haugen Basin Executive** responds that the Kova precedent is inapposite. Kova was transitioning from Reach administration to Sovereignty membership — a bilateral negotiation between the two hegemons. The Vaanthi are not transitioning. They are petitioning for commercial access while remaining under Reach administration. This is not a membership question or a transition question. It is a question about whether the Circuit system extends commercial privileges to populations that are, in political terms, subjects of the other hegemon.

The Haugen Executive states plainly that this is a strategic question, not a commercial one, and should be referred to the Defense Standing Committee.

**Peloro Basin Executive** disagrees with the referral. The Defense Standing Committee assesses military risk. The Vaanthi petition carries no military risk. It is a request for observer access to pricing data and quality certification standards — information that, the Peloro Executive notes, the Reach's own Civil Registry publishes selectively in its administered-territory trade bulletins. The Circuit would be providing, through a different channel, information the Vaanthi already partially possess through the Reach's own publications.

The Peloro Executive adds: the interesting question is not whether the Vaanthi should have observer access. It is why the Vaanthi are asking *us* instead of their own administration.

**Secretariat Deputy** responds to the Peloro observation. The Secretariat's assessment — informed by the Research Directorate's first-century review circulated to Basin Executives in Year 98 SP — is that the Vaanthi petition reflects a structural gap in the Reach's administered-territory framework. The Reach's classification system does not have a mechanism for administered territories to petition for reclassification. The associate-status transition pathway is discretionary and initiated by the Civil Registry, not by the territory. The Vaanthi, having developed institutional capacity that exceeds their classification, have no avenue within their own framework to request recognition of that development.

The Secretariat Deputy states: the Vaanthi are petitioning us because they cannot petition the Reach. We are the available institutional surface.

**Reach Civil Registry Liaison** requests permission to respond. The Chair notes that the Liaison has observer status and grants the floor.

The Liaison states that the Civil Registry is aware of the Vaanthi petition and that the Office of Administered Territory Affairs is conducting an internal review of the administered-territory framework's classification criteria. The Liaison does not characterize the outcome of that review. The Liaison states that the Registry would view Commerce Council action on the Vaanthi petition as unnecessary in light of the ongoing internal review, and notes that Commerce Council engagement with a Reach-administered territory's commercial access creates a precedent that complicates the bilateral framework governing hegemon-administered commercial relations.

The Liaison asks the Committee to defer consideration until the Registry's internal review is complete.

**Chair** asks the Liaison what timeline the Registry projects for the internal review.

The Liaison states that the review has no announced timeline.

**Chair** thanks the Liaison.

---

## Committee Discussion, Second Round

**Kova Basin Executive** states that the Committee should not defer. Four years is sufficient waiting. The centenary is the appropriate moment. The Kova Executive proposes: grant observer access to the Vaanthi Basin Commercial Association under the Thalassa Protocol, effective upon ratification by the Commerce Council plenary. Observer access confers no voting rights, no membership privileges, no tariff reductions. It confers access to the Circuit Standards library and attendance at the annual Basin Commercial Committee open sessions.

**Haugen Basin Executive** states that the form of the response matters less than the signal. Granting observer access signals that the Commerce Council considers itself a venue for administered-territory commercial grievances. This is a political signal, not a commercial one, regardless of what the protocol technically provides.

**Peloro Basin Executive** responds: every commercial decision is a political signal. The question is whether the signal is one we wish to send. The Peloro Executive's view is that it is. The Strange Peace is one hundred years old. The bilateral framework that governs hegemon-administered relations was designed for the first decade. The world has changed. The Vaanthi petition is a symptom of that change. Refusing to act because acting might send a signal is itself a signal — one that says the Circuit system is bound by the political constraints of an earlier era.

**Secretariat Deputy** notes, for the record, that the Secretariat has no recommendation on the merits of the petition. The Secretariat's role is to ensure the Committee has the information necessary to make a decision. The Secretariat observes that whatever the Committee decides, the centenary public statement should reflect the decision coherently. If the Committee grants observer access, the centenary statement should frame the Circuit as an inclusive commercial institution. If the Committee defers or denies, the centenary statement should frame the Circuit as a bilateral-respecting institution. Either framing is defensible. Both cannot be delivered simultaneously.

**Chair** calls for a preliminary sense of the Committee.

The Committee's preliminary sense is:
- Two executives favor granting observer access (Kova, Peloro)
- One executive favors referral to Defense Standing Committee (Haugen)
- One executive is absent (Laamu, sending regrets)

The Chair notes that the Committee cannot recommend plenary action without a majority of attending executives. The Chair proposes that the Committee defer final recommendation to the fourth session, with an invitation extended to the absent Laamu executive, and that the Secretariat prepare a briefing note on the Thalassa Protocol's observer provisions for distribution before that session.

The Committee assents.
