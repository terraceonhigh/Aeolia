# Aeolian Non-Staple Crops Reference

Non-staple crops provide worldbuilding texture. They do not drive the energy budget directly but shape trade, prestige, and the cultural feel of civilizations. Each named crop stands for a whole category of similar plants on that archipelago.

Names are drawn from the historical trade languages that moved these goods — Arabic, Sanskrit, Polynesian, Greek, Persian, Malay, Turkish, Formosan Indigenous, Ainu, and Ryukyuan/Okinawan. The naming itself tells you the trade history.

## V1 Canon (from sim_proxy.py)

These names are already established in the simulation's commodity system.

### Stimulants

| Name | Etymology | Staple crop association | Notes |
|------|-----------|----------------------|-------|
| **Qahwa** | Arabic *qahwa* (coffee) | emmer | Brewed from dried leaves; socially ritualized. Qahwa houses are where merchants negotiate and dissent ferments. |
| **Char** | Chinese 茶 *chá* (tea) | paddi | The stimulant of rice-growing archipelagos. |
| **Awa** | Polynesian *kava* (kava) | taro | Ceremonial drink, sedative rather than stimulant. Social bonding function. |
| **Pinang** | Malay *pinang* (betel nut) | sago | Chewed with lime. Mild stimulant, stains teeth red — a visible cultural marker. |
| **Aqua** | Latin *aqua vitae* (distilled spirit) | papa | Distilled from potato-analog tubers at higher tech levels. |

### Fibers

| Name | Etymology | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Kapas** | Sanskrit *karpāsa* (cotton) | Raw kapas is cheap bulk; kapas cloth is the first manufactured luxury. The spread between raw and finished is where industrial inequality forms. |
| **Seric** | Latin *sericum* (silk) | Animal fiber (silkworm), not a crop per se. The prestige textile. |
| **Byssus** | Greek *byssos* (fine linen/flax) | Plant fiber for cloth. Cooler-climate alternative to kapas. |
| **Fell** | Old English *fell* (animal skin/hide) | Leather and fur. Cold-climate trade good. |
| **Tapa** | Polynesian *tapa* (bark cloth) | Beaten from inner bark. No loom required — pre-textile cloth. |
| **Qivu** | — | Warm-climate fiber variant. |

| **Aes** | Latin *aes* (copper/bronze) | Functional prestige — tools, weapons, artifacts. |

## Fruits

| Name | Etymology | Climate | Role |
|------|-----------|---------|------|
| **Vinnu** | From Latin *vīnum* (wine) | Mediterranean volcanic slopes | Grape analog. Fresh fruit local; dried (raisins) travel well; fermented vinnu is wine — the prestige drink that competes with tava. Wine cultures vs. beer cultures is a real axis of civilizational texture. Volcanic soil is ideal for vinnu (Santorini, Etna, Canaries). |
| **Naranj** | Persian/Arabic *nāranj* (bitter orange) | Warm coastal lowlands | Citrus analog. Fresh doesn't travel; preserved naranj (marmalade, dried peel, oil) is minor luxury. Citrus prevents scurvy — a subtle tech multiplier for maritime expansion. |
| **Kelapa** | Malay *kelapa* (coconut) | Tropical low coasts | Meat, milk, oil, fiber (coir), shell. A complete subsistence package. Kelapa oil competes with tilu and saak in the oil trade. |
| **Zaytun** | Arabic *zaytūn* (olive) | Dry rocky semi-arid slopes | Oil, cured fruit, wood. Cultivation requires long-term land tenure (7–10 years to produce). Rewards settled, property-oriented societies. |
| **Tama** | Loosely from Nahuatl *tomatl* | Everywhere warm | Generic garden fruit. Nutritionally necessary, economically invisible. The sardai of produce. |

## Oil & Dye Crops

| Name | Etymology | Climate | Role |
|------|-----------|---------|------|
| **Tilu** | Tamil *tiḷ* (sesame) | Warm lowlands | Vegetable oil analog — cooking, lamps, medicine, waterproofing. Competes with saak oil in warm-water archipelagos. Cultivated, not wild-caught — more reliable, less valuable. A tilu polity doesn't need saak trade, structurally more Inward. |
| **Nili** | Sanskrit *nīlī* (indigo) | Warm lowlands | Dye crop. The only way to make kapas cloth worth luxury markup. Nili and kapas are economically symbiotic — one creates the other's value. A polity with both has a proto-industrial textile economy. |
| **Marich** | Sanskrit *marīca* (black pepper) | Wet-tropical | King of spices. Tiny, lightweight, keeps forever. A pinch worth its weight in bakala stockfish six hops away. The commodity that makes the relay trade layer profitable enough to sustain intermediaries. |

## Fermentables

| Name | Etymology | Climate | Role |
|------|-----------|---------|------|
| **Tava** | From Polynesian *kava* (loosely) | Universal | Starchy root that ferments into Aeolia's alcohol. Tava wine, tava beer, distilled tava spirit at higher tech. Culturally universal, doesn't shape politics — politics shapes tava. |

## East Asian & Indigenous Layer

### Formosan Indigenous

| Name | Etymology | Climate | Role |
|------|-----------|---------|------|
| **Djulis** | Paiwan *djulis* (Chenopodium formosanum) | Mid-altitude subtropical | Taiwanese Indigenous pseudo-cereal (like quinoa). Brilliantly colored (red, orange, gold), labor-intensive. The prestige grain — used ceremonially, brewed into wine. Signals agricultural sophistication and ceremonial complexity. Luxury trade good: low volume, high markup, short range. |

### Ainu

| Name | Etymology | Climate | Role |
|------|-----------|---------|------|
| **Pukusa** | Ainu *pukusa* (wild garlic / alpine leek) | Cold mountain | One of the most important gathered mountain plants in Ainu foodways. Preserved for year-round use. Marks cold-mountain archipelagos where cultivation is marginal but wild gathering is rich. Polities relying on pukusa-type foraging resist agricultural intensification and stay culturally distinct longer. |

### Ryukyuan / Okinawan

| Name | Etymology | Climate | Role |
|------|-----------|---------|------|
| **Goya** | Okinawan *gōyā* (bitter melon) | Subtropical | Signature vegetable of Ryukyuan cuisine. Represents a food culture shaped by trade exposure rather than indigenous agriculture — a marker of cosmopolitanism. Likely introduced from Southeast Asia. |
| **Ukon** | Okinawan *ukon* (turmeric) | Subtropical | Spice/medicinal crop. The subtropical-climate spice alongside tropical marich. Served with awamori (Ryukyuan spirit). |
| **Kokuto** | Okinawan *kokutō* (brown sugar, from sugarcane) | Warm-wet lowlands | Sugarcane derivative. The first plantation crop historically — labor-intensive monoculture that rewards conquest and forced labor. A polity with kokuto cultivation has economic incentive to colonize. Narrative only — not in the energy simulation. |

## Vegetables (narrative only, no simulation hooks)

| Name | Etymology | Role |
|------|-----------|------|
| **Kabak** | Turkish *kabak* (squash/gourd) | Dried gourds are containers before pottery — water vessels, storage, instruments. Tech 0 infrastructure. |
| **Bawang** | Malay/Indonesian *bawang* (onion/garlic) | Universal flavor base. The ingredient that makes sardai paste worth trading. |
| **Hubz** | Loosely from Arabic *ḥubūb* (grains/seeds), used for legumes | Lentils, chickpeas, beans. Fix nitrogen in soil — the difference between sustainable agriculture and soil exhaustion. Protein complement to grain for inland polities without fisheries. |
| **Sabu** | Loosely from Malay *sayur* (vegetable/greens) | Generic leafy green. Nutritionally necessary, economically invisible. |

## Linguistic Geography

The full non-staple roster spans:

- **Arabic:** qahwa, naranj, zaytun, hubz
- **Sanskrit:** kapas, marich, nili, tilu
- **Polynesian:** tava, tapa, awa
- **Greek/Latin:** vinnu, stafili→vinnu, chrysos, aes, byssus, seric, aqua
- **Persian/Arabic:** naranj, zaytun
- **Malay/Indonesian:** kelapa, pinang, bawang
- **Turkish:** kabak
- **Chinese:** char
- **Tamil:** tilu
- **Nahuatl:** tama
- **Paiwan (Formosan):** djulis
- **Ainu:** pukusa
- **Okinawan/Ryukyuan:** goya, ukon, kokuto

Each name carries its civilizational context — the language of origin tells you which trade network brought that crop to the Aeolian world.
